Background & Experience
A Trial Lawyer Who Walks the Scene, Knows the Family, Masters the Law

Christian J. Faiella has spent his career doing one thing: giving a credible, compelling voice to people catastrophically injured by the negligence or recklessness of others. As a member of Gump, Faiella & Bugalski, LLC, he brings the resources and depth of a full-service firm combined with the focused intensity of a trial lawyer who personally prepares each case.

Other attorneys refer their most difficult, high-stakes cases to Chris because of his reputation for zealous advocacy, innovative legal preparation, and exhaustive factual investigation. His approach is direct: walk the accident scene, get to know the family, understand the medicine, master the law — then stand in front of a jury and tell the truth with authority.

That approach has produced multi-million dollar jury verdicts and settlements against some of the largest corporations and insurance companies in the country — including high-profile victories where other firms turned the cases away.

Licensed in Missouri since 1995, Chris is also admitted to practice before the United States Supreme Court, the U.S. Courts of Appeals for the Eighth and Tenth Circuits, and the Eastern and Western Districts of Missouri's federal courts. The Martindale-Hubbell Law Directory has awarded him its AV-Preeminent® rating — the highest peer-review rating given — reflecting both his legal ability and his professional standards.

Beyond the courtroom, Chris is a published author and frequent CLE presenter on personal injury, insurance bad faith, and trial practice — contributing to Missouri Bar publications and national practitioner texts used by attorneys across the country.

How I Approach Every Case
A Lawyer's Philosophy

Winning serious injury cases requires more than legal knowledge. It requires a commitment to understanding every dimension of the client's experience — and the preparation to prove it.

01

Total Case Preparation

Every case I take is prepared as if it will go to trial. That means walking the accident scene, understanding the physics, studying the medicine, and knowing the applicable law better than the other side. Preparation is not just the work before trial — it is the trial itself.

02

Know Your Client's Story

Jurors don't award justice to a case number. They respond to a human being whose story is told with honesty and clarity. I invest the time to understand who my client is — their family, their work, their life before and after the injury — and I make sure the jury understands it too.

03

Never Back Down from Insurance

Insurance companies count on claimants accepting less than they deserve. I have litigated insurance bad faith cases my entire career, and I know how they operate. When an insurer wrongfully denies a legitimate claim, that refusal can become the basis for additional recovery under Missouri law.

04

Master the Expert Witnesses

In catastrophic injury cases, the battle is often fought through experts — doctors, engineers, accident reconstructionists, economists. I have spent decades learning how to select, prepare, and examine experts effectively, and how to expose the weaknesses in the opposition's.

05

Federal Court Capability

Many Missouri plaintiffs' lawyers are limited to state court. My admission to both federal districts, the Eighth and Tenth Circuits, and the U.S. Supreme Court means I can follow a case wherever it needs to go — an important asset in complex multi-party and interstate cases.

06

No Fee Unless We Win

Serious injury cases are handled on a contingency fee basis. There is no upfront cost to you. I bear the expense of litigation and get paid only when I recover money for you. The free initial consultation is just that — free, with no obligation and no pressure.
