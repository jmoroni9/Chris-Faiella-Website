About Chris Faiella

Chris Faiella

Member, Gump, Faiella & Bugalski, LLC  ·  Missouri Personal Injury Attorney

For more than 30 years, I have dedicated my practice to one thing: fighting for people who have been seriously injured through someone else's negligence. I do not practice family law, criminal defense, or estate planning. I practice personal injury — and I have spent three decades becoming very good at it.

My clients range from working families hurt in car accidents on Missouri highways to collegiate athletes with career-ending injuries, from families who have lost a loved one in a wrongful death to businesses and individuals whose insurance companies wrongfully denied legitimate claims. What they all share is the need for an attorney who will work harder than the other side, know the medicine, master the law, and fight for everything they deserve.

Lawyers refer cases to me because they know I will handle them with the same commitment I bring to cases I originate myself. If you are an attorney with a serious injury case outside your practice area — a tractor-trailer wreck, a products liability case, a brain injury claim, or an insurance bad faith matter — I welcome the conversation.

I am a member of Gump, Faiella & Bugalski, LLC, a full-service firm with offices in Columbia and Moberly, Missouri. My personal injury clients have the benefit of a complete litigation support team behind them, while working directly with me throughout their case.

Referring a Case?

This page is for attorneys considering a referral or co-counsel arrangement. All inquiries are handled confidentially and promptly.

PI Attorneys — Overflow & Specialized Cases

I regularly accept referrals from personal injury attorneys who need an experienced trial lawyer on cases outside their bandwidth or requiring specialized expertise. Cases I most commonly receive on referral from PI firms:

  • Third-party insurance bad faith cases
  • First-party vexatious refusal claims (RSMo § 375.420)
  • Complex products liability — vehicles, equipment, consumer products
  • Cases requiring federal court or appellate experience
  • High-value TBI and catastrophic injury cases needing intensive expert preparation

General Practice Attorneys — Serious Injury Cases

Many of my best cases come from general practice, family law, estate planning, criminal defense, and other non-PI attorneys whose clients are suddenly involved in a serious injury matter. Cases I commonly receive from general practice attorneys:

  • Tractor-trailer and commercial vehicle accidents
  • Wrongful death — auto, product, premises
  • Catastrophic car accident cases with significant damages
  • Pedestrian and bicycle accident cases
  • Drownings, boating accidents, recreational product injuries
  • Medical malpractice and nursing home neglect
