Birth Trauma
Birth Trauma Settlement

A $6.5 million settlement in a serious birth trauma case involving injuries sustained during labor and delivery. Birth trauma cases require intensive expert analysis of obstetric and neonatal standards of care, causation, and the child's full lifelong care and treatment needs.

$6,200,000
Products Liability — Wrongful Death
Carbon Monoxide Deaths — Defective Boat Generator

Four people died of carbon monoxide poisoning aboard a yacht at the Lake of the Ozarks on Memorial Day weekend. Investigation uncovered a defective exhaust component with a failure rate as high as 23%. The manufacturer had switched to better materials years earlier but continued selling the defective part without a recall. A worldwide recall followed the $6.2 million settlement.

$5,500,000
Insurance Bad Faith
Insurer Denies Coverage — Excess Verdict — Bad Faith Claim

A man suffered serious injuries in a fall. The insurer denied coverage and refused to protect its insured. After the policyholder suffered an excess verdict, they pursued their own insurer for bad faith failure to settle. Settled for $5.5 million.

$4,500,000
Auto Accident — TBI & Spinal Injury
Ejection from Vehicle — Brain Injury, T-12 Burst Fracture

A man in sales was ejected from his vehicle in a highway collision, sustaining a closed head injury, T-12 burst fracture, and multiple rib fractures. The defense admitted liability but disputed TBI severity and cited pre-existing conditions to limit life expectancy. Extensive experts demonstrated full permanence of injuries. Settled $4.5 million prior to trial.

A $4.5 million insurance bad faith recovery arising from a car crash involving a pedestrian. The insurer's bad faith handling of the underlying personal injury claim gave rise to substantial liability beyond the original policy limits.

Driver Over Hours — Wrongful Death

Insurance Bad Faith — Wrongful Death
Boom Truck Death — Insurer Claims Equipment Was a Car to Deny Coverage

Four family members died when an onboard generator produced carbon monoxide in the sleeping quarters of a recreational vehicle. The RV lacked CO detectors and a proper vapor-tight seal between the sleeping and engine compartments. Settled for $2.1 million.

Insurance Agent Negligence
